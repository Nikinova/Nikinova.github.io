Get your bike out of the shed, let's get going!

As the sun starts to show up more often and for longer, the weather becomes nicer, making it the perfect time to dig out your bike! Don't let it waste for another spring and summer!

Before you go, though...

Remember to always use your helmet, yellow vest and lights, as they will quarante you a safe journey.

The Helmet

In case of an accident, this will protect your head. Make sure to buy it at an authorised shop, as not all helmets are high quality and up to the road standards.

The Yellow Vest

You wanna be visible on the road, right? Well, the yellow vest, jacket or a back cover will reflect back the lights of the cars, making you visible to other users of the road.

The Strobe Light

There are many different types of strobe lights on the market, but they all do the same thing - produce a regular, flashing light.

Skills you should know

There are certain skills you should know before going out, especially on the road. They are best practiced first in a safe place without cars and dangers.

Road Safety - Signaling

As you may noticed, you are not in a car, but same safety rules apply to you, cyclist! Learn the hand signaling as this will be extremely useful to tell other users of the road where exactly you want to go.

Changing puncture

Oh no, did your wheel started to lose some air? There could be many reasons for that, but before you start to examine it, get to a safe spot, like a parking lot or on the side of a pathway, then you can check. If you think its something more serious than a small hole, it might be better to bring your bike to a bike shop for repair.

Take off your wheel

Turn your bike upside down, it will be easier and take off the wheel. Depending on which wheel it is, it might be easy or hard job.

Examine innertube

The wheel itself consists of two parts. The innertube is what holds the air. Examine it to find the hole and if you can, clean it from all the dust.

Glue

Once you clean off the dust, you can take proceed - glue a piece of rubber onto the puncture to close it off. Wait 20 minutes.

Pumping

Now, I hope you have a pump handy. Put the innertube back on the wheel and then pump it up. Job done! You should be able to ride on.

Locking your bike

When you leave the bike alone, make sure to lock it. There are many people who would gladly take unattended bike, so better be safe than sorry! There are many types of locks - u-locks, cable, chains, and many more, but its up to a preference. So before you start leaving your bike, get yourself a lock.
